Our 6 Dimensions at Go-Live

D1
Location / Worksite
For ARS: Herring Cove or Karts worksite. For VT: community served (Hydaburg, Metlakatla, etc.). KTN is VT default — no tag required.
Revenue, till/cash, facility costs, site-specific payroll
D2
Tour / Product Line
Identifies the specific tour or product. Enables product-level P&L without separate GL accounts per tour type.
Tour revenue, COS, charter, deferred revenue, marketing
D3
Department REQUIRED ALL P&L
Tags every transaction to a cost center (CANOPY, FLIGHT-OPS, ADMIN, etc.). Enables departmental P&L, budget tracking, and variance reports for department managers and the CEO.
All income and expense accounts — required at entry
D4
Aircraft Type
VT only. Identifies Beaver (AvGas) or Otter (Jet). Enables per-aircraft revenue, cost, and maintenance reporting — critical for FAA compliance and lease reserve tracking.
VT aircraft revenue, fuel, maintenance, lease accounts
D5
Sales Channel
Contract (cruise ship), Independent (walk-up), Cancellation, or Discount. Replaces QB sub-accounts and the retired contra accounts 4250 and 4350.
Tour revenue, charter, retail sales, coffee stand sales
D6
Entity / Intercompany
Identifies related entities for cross-charges and shared costs. D6=SHARED replaces the QB 69900/Class 9 workflow — shared costs now post to the real GL account and IES handles the 70/30 ARS/VT split automatically.
Due-to/from, related party loans, shared cost accounts

Background & Reference

How IES Dimensions Work ▸
  • IES supports up to 20 custom dimensions with unlimited values, organized in up to 5 hierarchy levels. For ARS and VT we use 6 dimensions at go-live.
  • When entering a transaction, the coder selects the applicable value(s) from each relevant dimension at the line-item level — not just the header — so each line of a bill or journal entry can carry different dimension values.
  • Payroll entries (6100–6150) will receive their department tag automatically from the payroll system import — no manual entry required.
  • Dimensions are configured by an Admin under Settings → Dimensions in IES. Only Admins and certain limited-access users can create, edit, deactivate, or reactivate dimensions.
  • You can establish a dimension hierarchy by creating multiple levels within a dimension for further granularity in reporting.
Why Dimensions Replace QB Classes ▸
  • Multiple simultaneous dimensions — one transaction can carry Location, Department, Tour Type, Channel, and Entity tags at the same time, unlike QB's single Class field.
  • Hierarchical values — dimensions can have parent-child relationships for roll-up reporting (e.g. all VT station communities rolling up to "Station").
  • Balance sheet support — unlike QB Classes, IES dimensions work on balance sheet accounts too (intercompany, fixed assets, deferred revenue).
  • COA simplification — instead of maintaining 50+ sub-accounts for every combination of tour × channel × location, the COA shrinks to ~120 clean accounts and dimensions capture the detail.
  • For existing QuickBooks customers, the first IES dimension maps directly from QB Classes in your converted data.
Setting Up Dimensions in IES ▸
  • Navigate to Settings → Dimensions as an Admin user.
  • Add a new dimension by providing a name (e.g. "Department") and then add values under it (e.g. CANOPY, SANCTUARY, KARTS…).
  • Assign dimensions to accounts in the COA — this controls which dimensions appear during transaction entry for each account.
  • Mark dimensions as required where applicable — required dimensions must be filled before a transaction can be saved.
  • Dimensions can be imported in bulk via Spreadsheet Sync, which dramatically reduces setup time for high-volume operations.
  • Only Admins and certain limited-access users can create, edit, deactivate, or reactivate dimensions after go-live.
Reporting with Dimensions ▸
  • Profit & Loss by Dimensions — see net income broken out by Department, Tour Type, Channel, or any combination in real time.
  • Budget variance reports — compare actual vs. budget by department or cost center. This is the primary output for department manager reviews.
  • Custom reports — filter or group any report by one or more dimensions simultaneously. You can also export and build pivot-style reports inside IES for advanced analysis.
  • Intercompany reports — with the D6 Entity dimension, IES handles the 70/30 shared cost allocations and cross-charges automatically with elimination entries.
  • Consolidated reports — view ARS and VT together or separately using entity-level filters across all 152 accounts.
  • IES also provides AI-generated forecasts based on up to five years of historical data, with the ability to partition budgets by custom dimensions.
Why D3 Department is Required on Every P&L Entry ▸

The Department dimension (D3) is what enables departmental P&L reporting in IES. Without it, every transaction from every team posts into a single undifferentiated pool and the system cannot produce reports by department.

In practice this means department managers receive their own monthly P&L; budgets are set and tracked at the department level; variances (actual vs. budget) can be investigated by team rather than requiring manual breakdowns; and the CEO can see at a glance which departments are performing vs. over/under budget. This replaces the QB Class feature for cost center reporting.

For payroll entries (6100–6150), the department tag comes automatically from the payroll system import. For all other P&L transactions it is a required field at entry.

Summary: In QuickBooks, all shared costs between ARS and VT were routed through account 69900 with Class 9, then cleared monthly via journal entry using a 70/30 (ARS/VT) allocation. In IES, shared costs post directly to the real GL account and are tagged D6=SHARED — IES handles the 70% ARS / 30% VT split automatically. Account 6990 Shared Services retires at go-live. The QB intercompany accounts (12501/12502, 22501/22502) are replaced by the consolidated 1250 Due from Affiliates account with D6 entity tagging.

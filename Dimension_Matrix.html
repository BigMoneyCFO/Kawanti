Why Department (D3) is required on virtually every income and expense entry

The department dimension is what enables departmental P&L reporting in IES — the financial reports that show each cost center's revenue, expenses, and profit contribution separately. Without it, every transaction from every team posts to a single undifferentiated pool, and the system cannot produce reports by department.

In practice this means: department managers can receive their own monthly P&L, budgets can be set and tracked at the department level, variances (actual vs. budget) can be investigated by team rather than needing manual breakdowns, and the CEO can see at a glance which departments are performing vs. over/under budget. This is the same reporting layer that QuickBooks provided through its Class feature — IES provides it through dimensions instead.

For payroll entries (6100–6150), the department tag will come automatically from the payroll system import, so staff do not need to enter it manually. For all other P&L transactions, it is a required field at entry.
